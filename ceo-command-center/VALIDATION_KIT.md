- Run out of stock on bestsellers (missed sales 😭)

15 minutes on a call (Zoom/phone/Discord - whatever works) to hear:

**Who I'm looking for:**
Sellers doing $1,000+/month in sales who spend time managing inventory/orders.

**Interested?**
Drop a comment or DM me with:
- Your monthly sales range ($1k-5k? $5k-20k? $20k+?)
- Biggest pain point right now
- Best time for a quick chat this week

I'll reach out to schedule!

Thanks in advance - really appreciate any insights! 🙏

## 📞 Interview Script

### **Pre-Interview (via DM):**
```

- I'm just gathering feedback, not selling anything
- No wrong answers - honest feedback is most valuable
- I'll send you the lifetime access link when ready

Does [Day] at [Time] work for you?
[Zoom link / Phone number]

See you then!
```

---

### **Interview Questions (20 minutes)**

**Opening (2 min):**
```
Thanks for jumping on! Just to set expectations - I'm building a tool for Etsy sellers
and want to make sure I'm solving real problems. There are no wrong answers, and honest
feedback is way more valuable than being nice.

Can you start by telling me a bit about your shop?
- What do you sell?
- How long have you been selling?
- Roughly how many orders per month?
```

---

**Current Workflow (5 min):**

1. **"Walk me through what you do when you get an order."**
   - Listen for: manual steps, switching between tools, pain points
   - Follow-up: "What part of that takes the most time?"

2. **"How do you currently track your inventory?"**
   - Listen for: spreadsheets, mental tracking, Etsy only, other tools
   - Follow-up: "Have you ever run out of stock unexpectedly?"

3. **"Do you know which products are most profitable for you?"**
   - Listen for: yes/no, how they calculate it
   - Follow-up: "How do you factor in Etsy fees and shipping?"

---

**Tools & Budget (5 min):**

4. **"What tools or software do you currently pay for to run your shop?"**
   - Write down EVERY tool and price
   - Examples: Vela, eRank, Craftybase, Quickbooks, ShipStation
   - Follow-up: "What's your total monthly spend on tools?"

5. **"What's the most frustrating thing about [tool they mentioned]?"**
   - Listen for: switching between tools, missing features, complexity

6. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix ONE thing about managing your shop, what would it be?"**
   - This is THE money question - listen carefully
   - Follow-up: "How much time/money does that cost you per week?"

---

**Solution Validation (5 min):**

7. **"I'm building a dashboard that syncs with Etsy and shows:"**
   - All your inventory with low-stock alerts
   - All your orders in one place
   - Automatic profit calculation (after fees/costs)
   - Simple shipping tracking

   **"Would something like that be useful to you?"**
   - Listen for: genuine excitement vs polite yes

8. **"If it saved you 5-10 hours per week and helped you avoid stockouts, what would you pay per month for it?"**
   - Listen for: actual number
   - If they say "free", ask: "What if I said $29/month, too much or reasonable?"

9. **"What would make you actually USE this vs just signing up and forgetting about it?"**
   - Listen for: must-have features, integration needs

---

**Commitment Test (3 min):**

10. **"If I built this and it did [repeat their pain points], would you commit to trying it when it's ready?"**
    - If YES: "Great! I'll add you to the early access list. Can I get your email?"
    - If MAYBE: "What would need to be true for it to be a definite yes?"
    - If NO: "Totally fair - what would you need instead?"

11. **"Last question - do you know any other Etsy sellers I should talk to?"**
    - Get 2-3 referrals if possible

---

**Closing:**
```
This was super helpful, thank you! I'll keep you updated as I build, and I'll send
you early access as soon as it's ready. If you think of anything else, feel free to
email me at [your email].

Thanks again!
```

## 🎯 Success Metrics

**After 10-20 interviews, look for:**

### ✅ **Green Light Signals (Build it!):**
- 10+ people say they'd pay $29/month
- Common pain points across 70%+ of interviews
- People currently paying $50-100/month for multiple tools
- 5+ people commit to early access with email

### ⚠️ **Yellow Light (Pivot or adjust):**
- People like it but wouldn't pay more than $10/month
- Everyone has different pain points (no clear pattern)
- Existing tools are "good enough"
- Need features you can't build (complex shipping API, etc.)

### 🛑 **Red Light (Don't build):**
- Most people wouldn't use it even if free
- Existing free tools solve the problem
- No one is willing to pay anything
- "It's a nice to have" responses

## 📈 Next Steps After Validation

**If 10+ people commit:**
1. Send them all an email confirming you're building
2. Ask if they want to join private Slack/Discord for beta
3. Set deadline: "Launch beta in 4 weeks"
4. Build with them giving feedback weekly

**If not enough commits:**
1. Analyze interview notes - what did they actually need?
2. Pivot to solving the most common pain point
3. Do 10 more interviews with adjusted pitch
4. Don't build until you have commitments
